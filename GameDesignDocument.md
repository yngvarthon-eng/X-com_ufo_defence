## 20. Blackouts: Mind, Life, Planetary & Universal
### Overview
Blackouts are disruptive events that can affect individuals, populations, entire planets, or even the universe, adding tension and unique challenges to gameplay.

### Types of Blackouts
- **Mind/Individual Blackouts:** Characters or units may suffer memory loss, confusion, or temporary loss of control due to alien tech, psychic attacks, or trauma. Impacts mission performance, unlocks hidden storylines, or triggers recovery missions.
	- *Example:* A squad member is rendered unconscious by a psychic attack, requiring a rescue or medical mission to restore their abilities and uncover what happened during the blackout.
- **Life/Biological Blackouts:** Populations or ecosystems may be affected by alien-induced comas, stasis, or biological shutdowns. Players must investigate, cure, or prevent these events to save key locations or resources.
	- *Example:* An entire city falls into a mysterious sleep. Players must research the cause, deploy antidotes, and defend the area from opportunistic enemies during the crisis.
- **Planetary Blackouts:** Large-scale power outages, communication loss, or environmental collapse caused by alien sabotage, criminal activity, or natural disasters. Triggers special missions, impacts base operations, and alters global strategy.
	- *Example:* A planetary EMP disables all technology, forcing players to rely on backup systems and manual operations while fending off attacks and restoring order.
- **Universal Blackouts:** Rare, catastrophic events (e.g., galactic EMP, dimensional rift, cosmic anomaly) that affect all factions. Can reset technology, change alliances, or introduce new threats and opportunities.
	- *Example:* A cosmic anomaly disrupts all advanced tech, causing temporary alliances between human and alien factions to survive a greater threat.

### Gameplay Impact
- Blackouts create urgent, high-stakes missions and force players to adapt strategies.
- May temporarily disable tech, units, or bases, requiring creative problem-solving.
- Can reveal hidden threats, storylines, or opportunities for alliances and advancement.
- Some blackouts may have lingering effects, such as permanent changes to unit abilities, base layouts, or faction relationships.

### Blackout Mission Hooks
- **Rescue & Recovery:** Save affected units, civilians, or cities from blackout effects.
- **Investigation:** Uncover the source of a blackout, whether alien, criminal, or natural.
- **Defense:** Protect vulnerable locations during a blackout from opportunistic attacks.
- **Adaptation:** Operate with limited resources, tech, or information until the blackout is resolved.

## 19. Teams, Co-op & Networking
### Overview
Integrating team play, co-op, and networking expands gameplay possibilities, enabling players to collaborate or compete in a connected world.

### Features
- **Co-op Squads:** Players can form teams, each controlling different units, squads, or bases. Cooperative play encourages teamwork and shared strategy.
- **Online Multiplayer:** Real-time or asynchronous play with matchmaking, friend lists, and chat. Players can join forces or compete in special events.
- **Shared Resources:** Teams can pool resources, share intelligence, and trade technology to achieve common goals.

- **Leaderboards & Achievements:** Track team performance, global rankings, and unlock special rewards for top players or squads.

- **Joint Base Defense:** Teams must coordinate to defend multiple bases from simultaneous alien or criminal attacks, sharing resources and reinforcements.
- **Coordinated Assault:** Players launch synchronized attacks on high-value enemy targets, requiring timing and communication for success.
- **Rescue Operations:** Teams split up to rescue captured units or civilians in different locations, sharing intel and support.
- **Tech Race:** Compete or collaborate to recover alien technology before rival teams or criminal enterprises.
- **Global Events:** Large-scale missions where all online players contribute to world defense, with rewards based on collective performance.
- **Special Co-op Abilities:** Unlock combined attacks, shared research projects, or unique team-based upgrades only available in multiplayer.

### Hybrid Mission Types & Abilities
- **Infiltration & Extraction:** Missions begin in real-time as players sneak into enemy territory. If detected, the game shifts to turn-based combat for tactical engagements. Extraction may return to real-time for timed escapes.
- **Pursuit & Ambush:** Real-time chases across the map, switching to turn-based mode when an ambush is triggered or combat begins. Players can set traps or prepare defenses during the transition.
- **Base Defense:** Real-time management of base resources and personnel as an attack unfolds. When enemies breach defenses, the game enters turn-based mode for squad-level combat inside the base.
- **Dynamic World Events:** Alien or criminal activity may start as a real-time event (e.g., UFO sighting, sabotage) and escalate into a turn-based mission if the player chooses to intervene directly.
- **Hybrid Boss Battles:** Boss enemies may have phases that alternate between real-time (area effects, movement) and turn-based (targeted attacks, special abilities), requiring players to adapt strategies on the fly.

- **Hybrid Unit Abilities:**
	- Some units have abilities that function differently depending on the mode. For example, a scout may move quickly and gather intel in real-time, but provide vision or support actions in turn-based mode.
	- Enemy hybrids may use real-time stealth or speed, then switch to powerful turn-based attacks when engaged.
	- Player units can unlock hybrid skills, such as setting traps in real-time that trigger special effects in turn-based combat.

## 18. Hybrid Systems: Turn-Based & Real-Time
### Overview
Hybrid systems combine turn-based and real-time gameplay to create a dynamic and strategic experience.

### Gameplay Integration
- **Real-Time Segments:** Used for base management, global events, UFO movement, and world progression. Players make time-based decisions and respond to evolving threats.
- **Turn-Based Segments:** Tactical combat missions switch to turn-based mode, allowing careful planning, squad management, and use of abilities.
- **Hybrid Missions:** Some scenarios transition between real-time and turn-based phases (e.g., real-time infiltration, turn-based combat upon detection).
- **Player Control:** Options to pause or slow time during real-time segments for issuing orders, blending both styles smoothly.

### Spawning & Managing Hybrids
- **Hybrid Events:** Special missions or world events can trigger hybrid gameplay, such as a real-time chase that becomes turn-based when combat starts.
- **Dynamic Transitions:** The game engine detects context (e.g., enemy contact, mission phase) and switches modes seamlessly.
- **Hybrid Units:** Certain units or enemies may have abilities that function differently in real-time vs. turn-based, requiring adaptive strategies.
- **Resource Management:** Some resources (e.g., energy, cooldowns) may regenerate in real-time but are spent in turn-based actions, encouraging planning across both systems.
- **AI Adaptation:** Enemy AI adjusts tactics based on the current mode, providing consistent challenge and immersion.

### Benefits
- Appeals to both strategy and action-oriented players.
- Increases replayability and depth.
- Encourages creative problem-solving and adaptive playstyles.

### 2.X Gameplay Mode Decision (Real-Time First)
- **Primary direction:** If a full hybrid (turn-based + real-time) becomes too complex, the game defaults to **real-time squad tactics**.
- **RT style:** Real-time with **pause and/or time-slow** for issuing orders (keeps tactical readability without building full turn logic).
- **Hybrid as optional layer:** Hybrid transitions can exist as a **stretch goal** or for specific modes/events, but the core game must remain fun and complete in RT-only.

### 2.X Squad Size (Variable, Multiplayer-Friendly)
- **Baseline squad size:** Minimum **4** units for standard missions.
- **Variable squad size:** Squad size can change by mission type, transport capacity, difficulty settings, and multiplayer player count.
- **Multiplayer assumption:** In co-op, each player can control a subset of units (e.g., 1–3 each) and squads scale naturally with more players.
- **Balance principle:** The UI/controls should support variable squads without rewriting mission rules (avoid hard-coding “exactly 4”).

### 2.X “Info-Box” (Temporary Player Thought UI)
The Info-Box is a temporary, in-game UI element presented as the **Commander’s inner monologue**. It surfaces the player’s current *intent* and gently pushes the game forward.

- **Purpose:**
	- Turn complex systems into clear next steps (tutorialization without stopping the action).
	- Provide “what now?” guidance during slow moments.
	- Introduce and unlock gameplay features in a paced way.

- **What it can show (content types):**
	- **Next objective:** “Investigate crash site”, “Return to base”, “Secure alien artifact”.
	- **New feature prompt:** “You can now build a radar dish”, “Research unlocked: X”.
	- **Intel lead:** framed as the Commander connecting dots: “NIA flagged unusual signals… it’s not random.”
	- **Post-event reflection:** short summary after a blackout/encounter that frames the next decision.

- **Voice & tone (v1):**
	- Short, tactical, slightly tense. The Commander is competent and focused.
	- Avoid comedy; avoid long exposition.
	- **POV rules:**
		- **First-person** ("I"/"we") for responsibility, reflection, and strategy framing.
			- Example: “We’re stretched thin. I need coverage before we commit.”
		- **Second-person** ("you") for immediate, actionable directives during play.
			- Example: “You’re overextended. Pull back and regroup.”
		- Keep both voices consistent: empathetic, brief, and never accusatory.
	- Examples:
		- “We don’t have enough coverage. A radar dish buys us time.”
		- “If this is a decoy, they want us to chase it. We need proof.”
		- “Four operators minimum. Any less and we start losing options.”

- **When it appears (simple triggers):**
	- First-time moments (first UFO detected, first injury, first loot recovered).
	- Stalls (player idle too long; no mission selected; resources capped).
	- World events (blackout begins/ends; NIA message arrives; base attack warning).

- **Design rules (to avoid annoyance):**
	- It should be **short**, skippable, and not block input.
	- Prefer a **queue** (one thought at a time) over spamming multiple popups.
	- Player can **dismiss/minimize**; the latest item remains accessible in a log.

- **Integration over time (v2+):**
	- Additional sources can be layered in later as optional “voices” feeding the same system:
		- **NIA** (external intel), **Base AI** (systems/optimization), **Squad radio** (ground truth).
	- Rule: keep the Commander voice as the default narrator, with other voices as tagged variants (never overwhelming the player).

## UFO Systems

### Overview
UFO systems are central to the gameplay, providing dynamic threats, mission hooks, and strategic challenges. They encompass the logic, behaviors, and mechanics for alien craft, their movement, abilities, and interactions with the world and player.

### Key Features
- **Instant Movement & Teleportation:** UFOs can move rapidly or teleport, requiring players to anticipate and react quickly. This mechanic creates unpredictable threats and forces investment in advanced detection and interception tech.
- **Invisibility & Stealth:** Some UFOs can become undetectable, using cloaking or stealth technology. Players must research and deploy specialized detection systems to counter these threats.
- **Multiple UFOs & Factions:** The game supports simultaneous incursions by multiple UFOs, each with unique strategies, abilities, and mission objectives. Factions may coordinate attacks, exploit weather, or form alliances with criminal enterprises.
- **Dynamic Spawning & Movement:** UFOs spawn and move based on global events, player actions, and AI strategies. Their patterns adapt to player defenses, weather, and intelligence operations.
- **Hybrid Abilities:** Certain UFOs possess abilities that function differently in real-time and turn-based modes, such as rapid movement in real-time and powerful attacks in turn-based combat.
- **Weather Interaction:** UFOs may exploit meteorological conditions (storms, fog, rain) to evade detection or gain tactical advantages.
- **Rank & Hierarchy:** UFOs have ranks (Scout, Commander, Captain, General) that affect their abilities, mission impact, and rewards for defeating them.

### Gameplay Integration
- **Detection & Interception:** Players use radar, satellites, and intelligence assets to detect UFOs. Successful detection triggers interception missions, which may be real-time or turn-based.
- **Mission Types:**
  - Interception: Engage UFOs in combat or force them to retreat.
  - Investigation: Track stealthy UFOs or those exploiting weather.
  - Sabotage: Disrupt UFO operations or alliances.
  - Recovery: Retrieve alien tech from downed UFOs.
- **AI Behavior:** UFOs adapt to player strategies, escalating difficulty and introducing new tactics. Boss UFOs and special units require advanced planning.
- **Rewards & Consequences:** Defeating high-ranking UFOs unlocks new tech, story missions, and changes faction dynamics. Failure to intercept may trigger blackouts, crises, or enemy alliances.

### Example Scenarios
- A fleet of stealth UFOs teleports across the globe, disabling detection systems and launching coordinated attacks. Players must upgrade tech and deploy squads to counter the threat.
- A high-ranking UFO exploits a storm to evade interception, forcing players to use meteorological stations and prediction systems to track and engage.
- Multiple UFO factions coordinate an assault, requiring players to manage resources, intelligence, and alliances to defend key locations.
